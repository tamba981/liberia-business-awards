Green Farms Liberia: Cultivating National Growth

Agriculture Business of the Year 2024

The Journey

What began as a small family farm in rural Bong County has blossomed into one of Liberia's leading agricultural enterprises. Founded in 2018 by James T. Morris, Green Farms Liberia started with just 5 acres of land and a vision to revolutionize sustainable farming in Liberia.

"We saw the potential in Liberia's fertile soil and favorable climate," says Morris. "But we also recognized the challenges facing local farmers - limited access to modern techniques, inadequate infrastructure, and difficulty reaching broader markets."

The LBA Impact

Winning the Agriculture Business of the Year award in 2024 proved to be a transformative moment for Green Farms Liberia. The recognition brought immediate credibility and opened doors that had previously been closed.

"Within weeks of winning the award, we received partnership inquiries from international buyers who had been hesitant to work with Liberian agricultural producers," Morris explains. "The LBA award served as a quality assurance stamp that validated our operations and standards."

"Winning the Liberia Business Awards gave us instant credibility. We went from a local farm to a nationally recognized brand overnight. The award opened doors we didn't even know existed."

- James T. Morris, Founder & CEO

Expansion and Innovation

Post-award, Green Farms Liberia embarked on an ambitious expansion plan:

  • Established partnerships with 8 international buyers in Europe and North America
  • Implemented advanced irrigation systems across 200+ acres
  • Launched training programs for local farmers in sustainable practices
  • Introduced organic certification processes
  • Developed value-added products including processed foods

Community Impact

Beyond business growth, Green Farms Liberia has made significant contributions to local communities:

  • Provided stable employment for 45 full-time staff
  • Supported 500+ smallholder farmers through training and market access
  • Invested in local infrastructure including roads and water systems
  • Established scholarship programs for farmers' children

Future Vision

Looking ahead, Green Farms Liberia aims to become West Africa's leading sustainable agriculture enterprise. Their five-year plan includes:

  • Expanding to 1,000 acres under cultivation
  • Establishing processing facilities for export-ready products
  • Developing an agricultural technology division
  • Creating a farmer cooperative model for nationwide impact
