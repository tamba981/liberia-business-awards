Pioneering Women Entrepreneurs

Sarah K. Johnson

Founder, Green Fields Agro

Transformed a small family farm into a $2M export business, creating 150+ jobs for rural women. Winner of LBA 2024 Agriculture Excellence Award.

"We started with just 5 acres and a dream. Today, we export to 3 countries and have trained over 200 women in sustainable farming."

Patience M. Cooper

CEO, TechWomen Liberia

Built West Africa's first women-focused tech incubator, training 500+ young women in coding and digital skills. Secured $1M in venture funding.

"Technology is the great equalizer. We're ensuring Liberian women aren't just users of tech, but creators and innovators."

Grace T. Roberts

Founder, Liberian Fashion Collective

Revitalized traditional Liberian textile arts, creating a luxury fashion brand that showcases at Paris Fashion Week. Employs 85 local artisans.

"Our textiles tell stories. Each piece carries the history and spirit of Liberian craftsmanship to the world stage."

Overcoming Challenges

"Being a woman entrepreneur in Liberia means facing unique challenges—access to funding, societal expectations, balancing family and business. But it's these very challenges that make our success stories so powerful. We're not just building businesses; we're building a new narrative for Liberian women in leadership."

— Mary B. Thompson, President, Women Business Association of Liberia
