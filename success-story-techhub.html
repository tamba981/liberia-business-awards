The Beginning

Founded in 2022 by Sarah P. Johnson, TechHub Liberia emerged from a simple observation: while Liberia had talented young people passionate about technology, there were limited opportunities for them to develop and apply their skills locally.

"We started with a small coding bootcamp in a rented room in Monrovia," recalls Johnson. "Our goal was to create a space where young Liberians could learn cutting-edge technology skills and apply them to solve local challenges."

The LBA Catalyst

Winning the Technology Innovation Award in 2024 served as a powerful validation of TechHub Liberia's approach and potential.

"The award came at a critical time," says Johnson. "We were seeking investment to scale our operations, and the recognition from LBA gave investors the confidence they needed. Within three months of winning, we secured $500,000 in funding from both local and international investors."

"The LBA win was a game-changer for investor confidence. We secured funding that allowed us to scale our operations and serve major corporate clients across West Africa."

- Sarah P. Johnson, CEO

Innovation and Growth

Post-award achievements include:

  • Development of Liberia's first locally-built e-commerce platform
  • Partnerships with 15 major corporations for digital transformation
  • Training programs that have graduated 200+ software developers
  • Launch of fintech solutions serving rural communities
  • Expansion to Ghana and Sierra Leone

Impact on Liberia's Tech Ecosystem

TechHub Liberia's success has created ripple effects throughout the country's technology landscape:

  • Inspired 5 new tech startups founded by TechHub alumni
  • Increased female participation in tech from 15% to 40%
  • Developed solutions used by 50,000+ Liberians
  • Created a pipeline of tech talent for international companies

Future Roadmap

TechHub Liberia's vision for the future includes:

  • Establishing West Africa's premier tech innovation center
  • Launching a venture fund for African tech startups
  • Developing AI solutions for agriculture and healthcare
  • Creating 1,000 tech jobs in Liberia by 2028
